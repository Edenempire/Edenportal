Life & trajectory

1999–2006 : early years – Born at Marianridge Hospital to Zamandosi Cele. Raised at Hammarsdale (Mpumalanga Township, Unit 2, Sompisi Rd, B1605). Attended Siphamandla Crèche under Gogo Ngidi. Started Grade R at Ntendeleni Primary School, then relocated with mother to Clermont. Forced to repeat Grade R at John Wesley Primary (due to birth after June cutoff) – sparked early awareness of institutional absurdity. Quickly absorbed English at John Wesley. Noticed “white flight” as school demographically shifted – an early lesson in social dynamics.

2007–2012 : Charles Hugo Primary School (coloured school) – Transferred after brief St. Teresa’s stint. Excelled academically; taught division by mother and sister Mbali before class introduction – teacher astonished. This forged Olama’s lifelong principle: “always stay ahead, and go beyond the syllabus.” Won a few achievement awards whilst there. Moved to Shallcross Primary (Grade 6–7) after mother bought first house at Savannah Park, Pinetown.

2013–2017 : Marklands Secondary – ‘the prison years’ – Forced into under-resourced school when mother couldn't secure Wingen Heights Secondary School. Olama mentally checked out, attended only for His mother’s sake. Grade 8 he passed okay; Grade 9 excellently (only because of Mr N. Govendor, form teacher & maths). Grades declined deliberately as silent protest. Met love of his life there as well, Nonhle Ngidi (even though relationship was for ~6 months) – He still holds deep affection for her. Matric 2017: intentionally failed, felt liberated. “I knew I’d be OK once I left Marklands. It was just all about leaving that school for Me”

2018 : gap year – full self‑reflection – No work, no study. Dedicated a full year to introspection. Then 2019 enrolled at Richfield. Quickly grew frustrated with overemphasis on communications modules that was basically like the english subject at high school, but yet Olama passed his IT course over there with his buddies. Used the qualification to land a 9‑to‑5 at Altice Optimum (Umhlanga) – which was a Covid‑hit role, and left after 10 months.

2021 : Rugby immersion – Devoted entire year to rugby skill development. 2022 – unlocked algorithms, self‑taught Python via Coursera; stacked certificates (HTML, JS, Java, Data Science, Data Analytics). Simultaneously worked at Capita (customer experience). Left August 2022 to pursue dev career. December 2022 hired as junior dev at U‑Connect (MVNO), soon became full‑stack but directionless – mentally disengaged to avoid contract extension.

2023 : Cape Town & Eden genesis – Autumn 2023, relocated to Cape Town to bootstrap own software company; returned to KZN sooner than planned. Teamed with Jedeye Moon (designer) on a project that stalled due to payment disputes. Pivoted to advanced JavaScript and, crucially, cryptography project for SANDF Defence Intelligence (ongoing). Met Thato; together launched EDEN – a parent conglomerate (software, security, aerospace, agriculture, law).

2024 : divine assignment & aerospace – Undertook a ‘secret military operation for God / Jesus Christ’ that permanently shaped his identity. Simultaneously dove deep into aerospace engineering. 2025 – Expanded EDEN: Arnold joined as chief supply chain officer. Performed 6‑month espionage‑grade market infiltration at Umhlanga to study target demographics, while maintaining SANDF work. 2026 – Shareholder, compliance officer, Tier 1 special operator (SA Airforce), bodyguard for EDEN’s Stainless Steel, computer scientist & aerospace engineer. And still plays rugby.
